Trichopoda pennipes

Trichopoda pennipes is a species of feather-legged fly in the dipteran family Tachinidae.





== Distribution ==

This species is native to North America (United States, Mexico), Hawaiian Islands and South America and has been introduced into southern Europe (France, Italy and Spain).





== Habitat ==

This species inhabits grasslands, hedge rows and crops where its key hosts are present.





== Morphology ==



Trichopoda pennipes can reach a length of 10.5 millimetres (0.41 in), about the size of a large housefly. These medium-sized flies have a velvety, black head. The black or brown thorax shows a few yellow stripes. Eyes are large, brown with yellow between. The color of the slender abdomen varies from bright orange to completely black. Females usually have a dark-tipped abdomen, while males have a dark orange apex. Wings are transparent, smoky with prominent veins.
